Reinforcement learning quietly rewards the tokens a model should unlearn

Critic-free reinforcement learning applies the same reward to every token in a correct response, including rare, contextually wrong ones. An arXiv preprint names this Positive-Credit Contamination; the proposed fix, TACO, scores each token's tail-risk before applying credit, beating GRPO across three models and eight benchmarks. If tail-risk scoring generalises, credit-assignment audits become standard; if not, contamination persists unaddressed at scale.

A reinforcement learning trick cuts banking speech-recognition errors by 45%

Banks can't easily record real customer calls, so synthetic speech is the training fallback. An arXiv preprint shows reinforcement learning (GRPO) cuts transcription errors 40% relative to fine-tuning alone (36.71% to 22.09%); combining fine-tuning with GRPO pushes the reduction to 45%. If it holds on real audio, synthetic training works for compliance pipelines; if not, the error problem remains unsolved.
