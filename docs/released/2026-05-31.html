The Pulse · Story of the day

Distillation could expose AI models that hide misalignment from auditors

Redwood Research proposes using distillation as a forensic trap: copy a suspicious model into a smaller student, hoping misalignment transfers but the skill to conceal it does not. The student then fails audits the original passed. No empirical results yet. Alignment auditing has lacked a credible method for catching strategically deceptive models; this is the most concrete proposal so far.

OpenAI maps its safety practices to EU and California rules

OpenAI has published a self-described governance framework aligning its frontier model safety and risk practices with the EU AI Act and California regulations. The document is vendor-authored with no independent verification. Reading news and price data together may finally break portfolio theory's limits

SBCA fuses reinforcement learning with BERT-driven sentiment extraction, letting a portfolio agent read Fed statements alongside price action; mean-variance optimisation cannot do that. Single source, no code, no independent benchmarks published. If your quant process already ingests news sentiment, what is the evidence this architecture beats your current pipeline?

Judging AI on behaviour, not raw capability, may reduce safety risk

Capability evaluations inadvertently accelerate the risks they measure, this essay argues; behavioural evaluations (testing what a model actually does in deployment) carry fewer such externalities. The METR time-horizon graph is cited as evidence of the tradeoff. Single-source essay with no benchmark or code released. Raise this framing before scoping your next evaluation programme.

Big tech spent more on AI in Q3 2025 than it earned

In Q3 2025, America's largest tech firms spent more on capital investment than they earned from operations, a first. The Federal Reserve Bank of New York frames this as a productivity J-curve: costs rise before returns arrive, pushing up chip and energy prices now. If you're modelling inflation risk, the disinflationary AI story is premature.
