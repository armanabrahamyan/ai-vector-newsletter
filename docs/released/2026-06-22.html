The Pulse · Story of the day

EU AI Act enforcement shifts from text to real institutions

The EU has seated a 60-member Scientific Panel and a 174-member Advisory Forum to oversee AI Act enforcement, and published a labelling code covering deepfakes and AI-generated public-interest content, with transparency obligations live from 2 August 2026. The Omnibus amendments softening high-risk deadlines remain unadopted. Governance here is no longer aspirational.

4 stories

Control is the product. Regulators, model builders, and researchers are all converging on the same problem: how do you keep an AI system inside its intended boundaries once real stakes are involved? The governance layer is now the competitive surface.

UK's financial regulator will enforce existing rules on AI deployments

The FCA will not write new AI rules; Consumer Duty and the Senior Managers regime apply now. Good-and-poor-practice guidance is promised later this year, drawn from an industry survey. Single-source blog post, no binding guidance yet. Which AI deployment decisions in your firm currently sit inside a named senior manager's personal accountability perimeter?

Losing real money cured a trading agent swarm of its hallucinations

A 20-month live experiment on Chinese equities replaced human graders with capital depletion as the reward signal. The swarm started at 6,700% portfolio turnover; it ended at a Sharpe ratio of 2.06. Single-source, no independent replication yet. If you're designing reward functions for trading agents, this is the methodology to pressure-test against.

Benchmark open models on your own tooling before you pick one

Hugging Face's harness measures not just whether an agent gets the right answer, but how much work it took: token cost, latency, failure rate across library revisions. A better CLI cut token usage up to 1.8 times in their own tests. Vendor-run benchmarks only; clone the harness and run it against your own tooling before you pick a model.

Research agents leak private data through their own search queries

Research agents combining private documents with web search leak sensitive facts through their query logs, even when explicitly told not to. MosaicLeaks shows training purely for accuracy nearly doubled leakage (34% to 51.7%). A privacy-aware training method cuts it to 9.9%. Single-source, vendor benchmarks; audit your agent's outbound query log before connecting it to proprietary document stores.

Five of seven AI models lost real money forecasting the future

PolyBench placed seven leading language models into live Polymarket prediction markets and tracked real dollars. Five lost money, all while expressing high confidence. Single-source, no independent replication yet. Google's parallel-text model is nearly as inspectable as its word-by-word predecessor

Google DeepMind audited DiffusionGemma and found its intermediate states readable enough to shrink the opaque computation gap from 28.6x to 1.1x versus a comparable standard model. Algorithmic transparency still lags. Single-source, pre-peer-review.
